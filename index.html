Developed with the Google Creative Lab.

Androidify started as a fun internal project to make the Android logo look like yourself. It has grown to become a key part of the global Android brand with over 10 million downloads.

Give it a try on your Android device! You can download it here.

Flow Home

A new kind of Android launcher.

Flow Home is a new kind of Android launcher that integrates social feeds into the home screen of your phone. We've also rethought interaction in the age of large screens by moving important UI down next to your thumb, and replacing awkward swiping with scrolling and searching.

Learn more and download at flowhome.co

AppChat

A chat room for every app on your phone.

AppChat is an Android app that automatically makes a chat room for every app you have installed. People are using it to get help with apps, compare high scores, and upload screenshots.

AppChat was built in a week, launched on reddit and went straight to the front page where it had over 10,000 downloads in the first two days. Read the behind the scenes story here, and you can download the app over at appchat.co.

Winken, Blinken and Nod

An iPad storybook with voice recognition.

Winken, Blinken and Nod is an iPad book for kids that listens to you read and animates the story to follow along.

All the art was created out of paper by former Martha Stewart Kids art director, Robin Rosenthal, then photographed and animated with real physics and lots of cute effects.

Winken, Blinken and Nod was selected by Apple as an in-store demo app and was on display in Apple stores across the United States.

SplinterNet

Android App

SplinterNet is an open source Android app that creates an unblockable Twitter like network that uses no cellular or Internet communications.

All messages are transmitted over Bluetooth between users, creating a true peer-to-peer messaging system. All messages are anonymous to prevent retaliation by government authorities. During times of civil unrest, governments have recently demonstrated a willingness to completely shut down Internet connections and cellular networks as a method of disrupting the organization of citizens. Additionally many governments filter politically-sensitive content, and spy on their citizens' network activity. SplinterNet provides a way to avoid government control of communications by creating a network with no central point of control.

Check out the source and contribute over at our GitHub project.

Slide Screen

An intelligent homescreen centralizing all your important information.

Slide Screen was our first major Android app and the first really different look at what an Android home screen could be.

Gizmodo called it “One of the most polished Android apps [they've] ever seen, it’s also in itself an argument for how great Android can be.”

It was retired in 2012 as the Android APIs underwent a major shift and some of the feature set was no longer possible. The original site is still up at slidescreenhome.com
